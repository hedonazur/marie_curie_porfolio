A time line of Marie Curie's life:

  • 1867 - Born in Warsaw, Poland as Maria Salomea Skłodowska.
  • 1883 - Graduates from school with a gold medal
  • 1985 - Worked as a home tutor for two years to pay for the expenses of her sister Bronislawa at the Flying University. She fall in love with the son of the family she tutored but is rejected by them for her lack of money.
  • 1887 - Enrols the clandestine Flying University, in Poland that admit women students.
  • 1890-1891 - She continued working as a tutor and began her practical scientific training in a chemical laboratory at the Museum of Industry and Agriculture.
  • 1891 - Enters the Sorbonne University in
  • 1894 - Marries Pierre Curie in Paris.
  • 1903 - Shares the Nobel Prize in Physics with her husband Pierre Curie and with a physicist Henri Becquerel.
  • 1911 - Wins the Novel Prize in Chemistry.
  • 1934 - Dies at the age of 66.
